Q&A

Three main differences between the Web versions:

Content:
In Web1.0, the content was static. it was one-way publishing of content without any real interaction between readers and publishers. In Web2.0, it started to act as a 2-way communication through social networking, blogging, etc. In Web3.0, AI and the web continuely learning what you want to consume and delivering you a personalized web experience.

Content Creators:
In Web1.0, Companies published content so that people can consume it. In Web2.0, people started to create and publish content so that other people can consume it. Furthermore, companies started to build platforms that let people publish their content in a more accessible way. In Web3.0, people also started to build applications that people can interact with. Companies have begun to build platforms that let people publish services by leveraging the associations between people.

Search Engines:
In Web1.0, search engines retrieve macro contents. The results were very general and not specific. Search is very fast but many times results are inaccurate. In Web2.0, search engines retrieve tags with micro contents like pictures, links, events, news, Blogs, audio, video, etc. The process of tagging is manual. In Web3.0, search engines will retrieve micro-content texts which were tagged automatically. The results are more precise.

Well demonstrate those differences with the next scenario:

Let's assume we want to search for "Alter Bridge" band. Now let's compare the results retrieved by the search engines. In Web1.0, the search engine retrieved us pages that include the words "Alter Bridge", maybe even only one of the words apears in it. The search engine doesn't really know if the content inside the pages related to our query. There is a high probability that the results will be inaccurate. In Web2.0, as we mention above, search engines retrieve tags with micro contents. we could expect to get pictures, links, events, news, Blogs, audio, and video related to our query. The results retrieved are more accurate and more varied.
